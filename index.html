20260323 EMEA Org Design Analysis FY26

A market-led EMEA org, anchored in the countries that matter most.

The plan lifts TAM coverage from 54.0% to 71.8%, adds focus around 1,502 whitespace accounts, and concentrates leadership attention in UKI, Germany, and France.

Read the exec summary Review the market map See key accounts

Exec Summary

Move EMEA from broad geographic coverage to a market-led operating model.

The recommendation is to anchor FY26 coverage in Tier 1 markets, add 111 recommended ADs across the planning model, and focus leadership inspection on the accounts and countries with the highest whitespace.

01

Put the most complete teams around UKI, Germany, and France.

These are the Tier 1 win markets and the largest coverage gaps. They should drive manager placement, AD hiring, specialist coverage, and account-level inspection.

02

Use hubs and regional coverage where density is thinner.

Tier 2 and Tier 3 markets still get explicit coverage motions, but the org should avoid turning every country / segment cut into a bespoke patch.

03

Tie org-design choices to must-win execution.

The account filter combines the market-planning list with the must-win command deck so each region and segment view ends in concrete accounts, owners, ARR, and pipeline.

Organisational Design

Market-first for priority countries. Geo-led where specialization is not yet dense enough.

  • Verticalize Large Enterprise / Enterprise first; keep Digital Natives and Mid-Market closer to the geo motion.
  • Use UKI / North, DACH & Central, and France & South as practical leadership groupings.
  • Close manager mapping, remote hiring, TS / XFN investment, partnerships, and field-announcement decisions before rollout.
